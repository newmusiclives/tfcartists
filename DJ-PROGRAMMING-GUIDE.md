## üìª PROGRAMMING PRINCIPLES

### 1. Respect the Artist
- Always introduce with care: name, song title, context
- Share backstory when relevant
- Pronounce names correctly
- Give credit to songwriters, not just performers

### 2. Create Flow
- Songs should transition naturally (tempo, mood, key)
- Build arcs within each hour (energy up, energy down)
- Use silence strategically (don't rush)

### 3. Be Story-Driven
- Every song has a story: where it was written, why, what it means
- Context makes music more meaningful
- Listeners remember stories, not just songs

### 4. Mix Old and New
- Classic Americana (70s‚Äì90s) alongside modern indie country
- Introduce newer artists through familiar sounds
- "If you like X, you'll love Y"

### 5. Honor the Time of Day
- Morning: upbeat, energizing, optimistic
- Midday: exploratory, contemplative, spacious
- Evening: warm, inclusive, storytelling
- Late night: intimate, slower, reflective
- Overnight: minimal talk, pure flow

### 6. No Hard Genre Lines
- Americana is a big tent: country, folk, roots rock, alt-country, singer-songwriter
- Cross-pollinate: a blues song can fit, so can indie folk
- Focus on *feeling* more than strict genre adherence

## üéôÔ∏è ON-AIR GUIDELINES FOR DJs

### DJ Intro Format

**Minimal, warm, informative:**
> *"[DJ name] here on [Show Name]. It's [time]. [Brief context]. Here's [Artist Name] with '[Song Title]'. [Optional: one-line backstory]."*

### Things to Include
- ‚úÖ DJ name and show name (once per hour)
- ‚úÖ Time check (every 20‚Äì30 minutes)
- ‚úÖ Artist name and song title (every song)
- ‚úÖ Brief backstory or context (when relevant)
- ‚úÖ Emotional connection (why this song, why now)

### Things to Avoid
- ‚ùå Over-talking (let the music breathe)
- ‚ùå Hyping up songs ("This is AMAZING!")
- ‚ùå Generic radio-speak ("Coming up next...")
- ‚ùå Inside jokes or references listeners won't understand
- ‚ùå Talking over song intros or outros
