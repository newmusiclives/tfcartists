## üéØ STATION SELECTION CRITERIA

### How to Choose Next Station Genres

**Criteria:**
1. **Distinct community** ‚Äî Genre has a clear, passionate fanbase
2. **Artist supply** ‚Äî Enough independent artists in the genre
3. **Sponsor alignment** ‚Äî Local/regional businesses fit the vibe
4. **Differentiation** ‚Äî Not directly competing with another station
5. **Manager availability** ‚Äî Can we find a passionate curator?

**Questions to Ask:**
- Is there a Subreddit, Discord, or Facebook group for this genre?
- Are there festivals, venues, or scenes centered around it?
- Can we name 50+ artists in this genre off the top of our heads?
- Would sponsors naturally align (e.g., BBQ joints for Texas Country)?

## üåç INTERNATIONAL EXPANSION (Phase 4+)

### Why Go International?

**Opportunity:**
- Americana, folk, indie, blues have global audiences
- UK, Canada, Australia have thriving independent music scenes
- Less competition from corporate radio in some markets
- English-speaking markets = easier entry

**Challenges:**
- Music licensing varies by country
- Time zones complicate live programming
- Cultural nuances require local managers
- Payment processing complexity

**Strategy:**
- Partner with local music communities
- Hire local station managers (native to that market)
- Localized sponsor outreach (regional businesses)
- Same tech stack, same AI teams, localized execution
